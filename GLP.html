Portfolio – Gur Ladizhinsky


Hello, and welcome to my portfolio! I’m a 21 years old CS student, and I’ve been making games as a hobby for around 9 years now. This portfolio will highlight my favourite ones.

Most of my games were made in Unity, but I’ve experimented with other engines as well – Godot especially is one I’ve grown increasingly fond of. My favourite genres (and thus the ones I have the most experience with) are platformer, strategy, and RPG games, but I’m fine with any genre really (except ultra-violent ones). I love story-based games and modding/romhacking other games.

Highlights


Frogman Magmaborn (Unity, Mostly Solo, August 2023)

  

Frogman Magmaborn is my largest project to date – an open-source rogue-like tactical RPG with a story. I’ve made most of the game on my own, but my friend Dan helped with the story, made the conversation backgrounds, and created some portraits (including the high-resolution ones used in the trailer).

In addition to the game itself, I’ve also created a pretty extensive modding tool in WinForms called Frog Forge, which I've used as well to make most of the content in the game.

The Cursed Vest of Soul Harvesting (Godot, Solo, January 2023)

  

This game features the best protagonist I’ve ever made – a mind-controlling vest. It’s a puzzle game where you need to climb a tower by possessing different kinds of monsters. It was made for Ludum Dare 52 Compo.

This was my 8th and personal favourite Ludum Dare submission, but some more highlights include:
Bad News Inc. (LD53 Compo), a game about delivering bad news which won 6th in humour;
Dilley and the Sacred Stones (LD50 Compo), a rhythm game and Infinity War parody; and
B.A. in Animal Psychology (LD49 Compo), a game where you talk to animals about their issues and then ride them.

Medieval Space Eel Collectors (Unity, Team, November 2022)

  

During my student exchange semester in Berlin, I participated in two Berlin Mini Jams, where we had to make a game in only 8 hours in teams. This game was my second one, made for the themes of Recycle, Medieval Space Traveling and Electric Eels.

I’ve programmed & written the event part of the game and made the music, while my teammates worked on the side-scrolling part and 3D models.

Project Climb (Heaps.io, Solo, June 2021)

  

After using Unity for 5 years already by this point, I wanted to try out other engines and programming languages, so I set out to study Haxe & Heaps.io while making a game using them, in only one month. The result isn’t spectacular, but I learned a lot from working on it.

The game itself is a co-op action-platformer where you have to climb an underground cavern, with a focus on different kinds of spells – the game allows each player to pick one of four elements, each of which features different ground and air attacks (inspired by Super Smash Bros.).

Gravity Banana: Part 1 (Unity, Mostly Solo, January 2019)

  

This game was actually a remake of the original Gravity Banana, a game I’ve worked on alongside my friend Dan back in 2016. It is an action-platformer game where you play as a gravity-changing banana which throws exploding chilli peppers at pickles. I made most of it, but Dan created all the concept art and he and a few other friends helped with the music and voice acting.

While it’s quite old by now (and its code was absolutely terrible), it was the first game I worked on which felt like a “real” game – it features 25 levels (including 5 boss battles), 5 worlds, 4 playable characters, and a co-op mode.

Disc-O-Key


Disc-O-Key is the small game dev team (mostly me and a friend) I make most of my larger projects with. It contains both my earliest works (like Boomby from 2014) and my latest large game (Frogman Magmaborn).
